About:
Tiny Fractals is a tool I (Dave; Hi!) wrote because I noticed a lot of people on sites like Reddit wanting to teach, and a lot of others wanting to be taught, but not a lot of cross-contact!
Tiny Fractals allows you to find people that either can teach you, or that you can teach!
Basic Usage:
I’ve tried to make usage of this app as simple as possible. Assuming you’ve logged in (i.e., you can see this page!), your first stop is gonna be the Profile page.
Profile:
  • Add skills you wanna learn by clicking the
     Add Skill
    button.
    • You can also add completely new skills if the one you want isn’t listed!
    • For each skill, you’ll be asked to give it a (somewhat subjective!) rating from 1-10. Be honest with yourself!
    • Feel like you can teach a particular skill? Go ahead and click that “Can Teach” checkbox!
  • Change any other profile info you want.
Now that we’ve told the world who we are, we wanna move onto meeting other people! Let’s head over to the Match page.
Match:
This is the crux of the app. Here, you can connect with mentors/students, search by skill, etc., and generally make those awesome educational connections. How you use this depends on whether you’re a mentor or a student:
 I wanna learn!
  • Start by picking some skills you wanna learn.
  • If you just wanna improve on a skill (and not start from scratch), you can optionally add min level.
  • Only teachers who have that skill, have clicked their own “Can Teach” box, and are at (or above) your min level will be included.
  • Once you've found a match, click "Connect" to send them a connection mail (you'll get a copy in your inbox too).
    • Note that some users are marked as "Demo User".
    • These are fake users that exist purely to show you what the app looks like.
    • They're auto-generated, with Math! And Science!
    • They don't actually exist, so they're not available for teaching (they wouldn't be very good at it!).
Can’t find any teachers that wanna teach your given skills? Go ahead and smack that
 Request Lesson
button, and we’ll put a message out into the Aether that you’re lookin for some help!
  • You can view your lesson request on the View Requested Lessons tab at any time up until you’ve connected with a teacher.
  • You can also delete this lesson request if you don’t need it anymore!
  • Each teacher that offers to teach this lesson will have an
     Accept
    button next to their name. Clicking this will delete the lesson request, and send a message to both Mentor and Student that it’s time to connect!
 I wanna teach!

Cool! If you wanna teach, there are two ways to get started. The first is to simply create a profile with some cool skills and remember to click that “Can Teach” box.
The second is to head on over to that View Requested Lessons tab. Here’s where you’ll see all the lessons that other users have requested (duh).
  • If you feel like you can teach a lesson, click that
    Teach It!
    button.
  • Don’t feel like you wanna teach that lesson anymore? We understand. Just click that
     Cancel Teaching Offer
    button. Note that this does not apply to lessons in progress (i.e., you must click this button before the student accepts your offer).
Tips:
Here’s some tips for general use.
  • Be honest about your skills! Remember this app is for people to learn, so if you say you’re a level 9/10 in Elvish, and you’re realistically only a level 2, it’s only gonna confuse people.
  • Be nice! Remember, everyone does better when we treat our students and teachers with respect and friendliness!
  • The more info you can provide, the better. Studies show that sticking a nice, friendly picture on your profile to help make yourself seem more approachable is highly recommended by Dave.
  • Try to include some projects too. They don’t need to be the next FaceBook, but the more people can see how you work, the better!
